Cancrous

Breast cancer is a disease that occurs when cells in the breast begin to grow abnormally and multiply uncontrollably, forming a lump or mass. These cancerous cells can invade nearby tissues and, in advanced stages, spread to other parts of the body. It most commonly starts in the milk ducts or the lobules that produce milk. Early detection through regular screening and timely treatment greatly improves the chances of successful recovery.

Not Cancrous

A normal breast without cancer is made up of healthy tissue that includes milk glands, milk ducts, and supportive fat and connective tissue. The cells in a healthy breast grow and divide in an orderly way, maintaining their natural structure and function. There are no abnormal lumps, growths, or changes in shape, and the breast feels soft and consistent in texture. In a normal, cancer-free breast, there is no uncontrolled cell growth or spread to surrounding areas.
